Research Interest Summary

Computer Science Theory Education

I am interested in investigating how students learn in computer science theory courses, with an emphasis on understanding the factors that contribute to their success and struggles. As of right now, my focus is on algorithm design, but I plan to extend this to automata theory and quantum computation. With many students finding algorithm design difficult, I want to discover how we can revise our teaching methods and help resources to ensure that students feel confident in their abilities to design algorithms. Where are they making mistakes? What mistakes do they make? Why are they making mistakes? How well do our class resources make progress towards eliminating these mistakes? Answering these questions is crucial if we want to improve how we teach algorithm design.


Hybrid Quantum-Classical Algorithm Design

While quantum computing has seen very promising results recently, our current devices are expensive and prone to noise. Running pure quantum algorithms on the noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices we have right now carries a high risk of outputting unusable results due to decoherence. With pure quantum algorithms being limited by noise, we instead approach algorithm design by finding ways to leverage quantum computing to enhance classical algorithms. Quantum frameworks are known for their speedups — what if we used them to mitigate the computational bottlenecks commonly associated with inefficient classical algorithms? How do we analyze the computational complexity of these hybrid algorithms?
